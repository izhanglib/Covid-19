Izzy's Professional Development during Covid-19 Lockdown

Graphic Design Courses

In a library setting, I may be asked to design a poster or a webpage. To find a way to improve my graphic design skills, I took the Fundamentals of Graphic Design, an online course organised by California Institute of Arts (CalArts). I learned the concept of denotation and connotation and earned some hands-on experiences in managing shape, colour, rhythm and pattern in the image-making process. By taking the Introduction to Typography, I learned that a selected typo looks good is an important, but the connotation of that specific typo also should be well considered. Additionally, I also briefly touched The Ideas from the History of Graphic Design, for example, the implications of Bauhaus in contemporary graphic design.

Professional Communication Courses

I attended three online courses organised by Georgia Institute of Technology (GeorgiaTech). By taking the course Write Professional Emails, I learned the high/low context communications as well as age and gender considerations when writing professional emails. By taking the course Speaking Professionally in Person, Online and On the Phone, I learned how to build a positive business relationship with a customer through small talk and elevator speech. I learned and set up a personal e-portfolio after taking the course Build Your Professional ePortfolio. I have a better understanding on the role of e-portfolio in professional networking and job hunting. I am better prepared to help a customer to set up his or her one in the future.

Auckland Libraries: Whakatipu i te reo Māori e-learning

I did two e-learnings - Auckland Libraries: Whakatipu i te reo Māori - Module 1: Whakahuahua/Pronunciation and Module 2: Ngā kupu ā Pātaka Kōrero to refresh what I learned in the night class at Unitec in 2018. By taking the e-learnings, I reviewed the pronunciation of te reo, including vowels, consonants, etc. and learned some new words in te reo in a library setting, such as whare pukapuka meaning library. Using translation apps, I also created my own version of mihimihi and recorded it in the audio track below. When I come back to Te Manawa, I am happy to present it in front of my colleages.

Auckland Libraries: Personal Safety and Security e-learning

Through case studies in a library setting, I learned the most appropriate response to a workplace safety and security incident, either resolve it or minimize the risk. I also learned New Zealand legislations concerning workplace safety and security. After taking the course, I have a better understanding on what an assault is, under what circumstance an action could be justified as self-defense and how much force is appropriate under a specific circumstance, as well as how to deal with a trespass incident. The course helped me to be better prepared when an workplace safety and security incident does happen.
